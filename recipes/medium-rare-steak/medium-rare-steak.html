Medium Rare Steak

The perfect medium rare steak has a juicy red center while avoiding chewiness and toughness. The ideal recipe involves a very hot pan, with even heating of both sides of the steak to produce a nice flavorful crust. This recipe also calls for creation of a butter sauce to enhance the flavor of the meat.

Ingredients

  • New York strip steak
  • Cooking oil, preferably vegetable oil
  • Unsalted butter
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Rosemary
  • Thyme
  • Sage

Steps

  1. Pat the steak dry and season both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a cast iron skillet with vegetable over medium-high heat on the stove.
  3. When oil is hot and has coated the pan, sear both sides of steak for 3-4 minutes or until a brown crust develops.
  4. Use tongs to render the fat and sear edges for 1-2 minutes each side, then drop heat to medium and add butter, garlic, and herbs.
  5. Spoon sauce over steak until they are just under desired temperature.
  6. Let steak rest for 10 minutes on cutting board loosely covered with tin foil. Slice and serve with extra butter sauce.
